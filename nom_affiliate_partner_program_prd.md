# PRD: Nom Affiliate & Implementation Partner Program

## Working Title

**Nom Partner Program**

Alternative names:

- Nom Affiliate Program
- Nom Restaurant Growth Partner Program
- Nom Certified Partner Program
- Nom Local Partner Network
- Nom Restaurant Systems Partner Program

This PRD focuses on the immediate money-making version of the program: affiliates and partners who help Nom acquire restaurants, set up Nom’s restaurant management system, activate services/apps, and earn commissions from the revenue they help generate.

The broader growth network — creators, waiters, QR sharing, consumer referrals, supplier loops, locality campaigns, loyalty rewards, and franchise growth — should be treated as future expansion. The foundation should be designed so those can be added later, but the first version must focus on one strong business engine:

> **People who know restaurants should be able to sell Nom, help restaurants get started, and earn real commissions.**

---

# 1. Product Vision

## 1.1 The Core Idea

Nom should create a partner program where motivated people can make money by bringing restaurants onto Nom and helping them succeed.

These people may be:

- local salespeople
- students
- freelancers
- restaurant consultants
- POS hardware sellers
- digital marketers
- web designers serving restaurants
- people who already know restaurant owners
- small agencies
- ex-restaurant staff
- suppliers who work with restaurants
- ambitious individuals looking for a side income
- future city/locality partners

The program should let them do more than simply share an affiliate link.

They should be able to:

- learn what Nom offers
- register as an affiliate/partner
- get approved
- access sales material
- pitch restaurants
- register leads
- book demos
- help close deals
- set up the restaurant’s Nom account
- configure menus, POS, inventory, QR ordering, staff access, and other modules
- optionally sell additional services
- track every restaurant they bring
- earn commissions from subscriptions, setup fees, add-ons, and services
- grow into higher partner tiers over time

The program should feel like a serious income opportunity, not a cheap referral gimmick.

The affiliate should feel:

> “If I can bring restaurants to Nom and help them get started, I can build a real income stream.”

The restaurant should feel:

> “This partner is not just selling software. They are helping me set up a system that can run my restaurant better.”

Nom should feel:

> “We are building a distributed sales and implementation network without hiring a full sales force in every locality.”

# 2. Main Business Goal

The immediate goal is to create a repeatable partner-led acquisition and onboarding engine for restaurants.

Nom wants to use the affiliate program to:

- acquire restaurants faster
- reduce dependency on only direct sales
- expand locality by locality
- let trusted local people sell Nom
- create a low-cost sales channel
- reward people only when revenue is created
- increase onboarding capacity
- make implementation less dependent on the core team
- create a future network of certified restaurant tech partners

The program should not only generate leads.

It should generate:

- qualified restaurant prospects
- signed customers
- completed setups
- activated modules
- subscription revenue
- service revenue
- upsell revenue
- retained customers

A normal affiliate program stops at “lead generated.”

Nom’s partner program should continue until:

> “The restaurant is live, trained, using the system, paying, and ready to expand into more Nom services.”

# 4. What This Program Is

## 4.1 It Is a Revenue Partnership Program

The program exists to make money for three parties:

1. **Nom** earns subscription/service revenue from restaurants.
2. **Restaurants** get a system that helps them operate and grow.
3. **Partners** earn commission by selling and/or implementing Nom.

The product should be designed around this triangle.

If any side fails, the program fails.

If partners earn but restaurants churn, Nom loses.
If restaurants benefit but partners do not earn clearly, partners stop selling.
If Nom earns but partners feel cheated, trust breaks.
If partners bring bad-fit customers, support suffers.

The system should reward partners for bringing restaurants that actually adopt Nom successfully.

## 4.2 It Is Not Just a Link-Based Affiliate Program

A basic link-based affiliate program is too weak for this business.

Restaurants are high-touch.

They may need:

- explanation
- trust
- demo
- pricing discussion
- menu setup
- staff training
- inventory configuration
- QR setup
- hardware help
- handholding during first few days
- follow-up after onboarding

So the partner program should support different levels of involvement:

- simple referral
- qualified lead submission
- sales-assisted referral
- co-selling
- full setup partner
- certified implementation partner
- reseller/agency partner

Partners should be rewarded according to the value they provide.

## 4.3 It Is a Pathway From Affiliate to Certified Partner

Someone may start casually:

> “I know one restaurant owner.”

Then they submit a lead.

Then they earn.

Then they realize:

> “I can do this again.”

Then they learn to pitch better.

Then they help with setup.

Then they become certified.

Then they manage multiple restaurants.

Then they become a locality partner.

The program should support this progression.

# 5. Program Participants

## 5.1 Affiliate

An affiliate is a person who brings restaurant leads or customers to Nom.

They may not perform setup.

They mainly:

- share Nom
- submit leads
- introduce restaurant owners
- help book demos
- track status
- earn referral commission

Best for:

- casual referrers
- people with restaurant contacts
- students/freelancers
- restaurant owners referring friends
- early-stage partners

## 5.2 Sales Partner

A sales partner actively sells Nom.

They do more than refer.

They may:

- identify restaurants
- pitch Nom
- explain pricing
- answer basic objections
- book demos
- follow up
- help close

They may not do full setup, but they influence the sale strongly.

Best for:

- freelancers
- local salespeople
- small agencies
- digital marketers
- people comfortable with field sales

## 5.3 Implementation Partner

An implementation partner helps restaurants go live on Nom.

They may:

- create restaurant account
- configure menu
- configure tables
- configure staff roles
- set up POS
- set up inventory categories/items
- configure QR menu
- test ordering flow
- train owner/manager/staff
- ensure first day usage works
- collect feedback

They may or may not be the person who sold the restaurant.

Best for:

- technically comfortable partners
- local service providers
- agencies
- people who can visit restaurants physically
- people who can follow setup checklists carefully

## 5.4 Full-Service Partner

A full-service partner both sells and implements Nom.

They can own the entire journey:

1. find restaurant
2. pitch Nom
3. register lead
4. book demo
5. close deal
6. set up system
7. train staff
8. support initial adoption
9. earn commission and setup income

This is the most valuable partner type.

The program should push serious partners toward this model.

## 5.5 Agency / Reseller Partner

An agency or reseller sells Nom as part of their own service offering.

Examples:

- restaurant marketing agency
- website agency
- local business consultant
- POS hardware vendor
- digital transformation consultant
- cloud kitchen consultant

They may bundle Nom with:

- website creation
- menu design
- social media marketing
- paid ads
- photography
- QR campaign setup
- WhatsApp ordering setup
- hardware installation

They need a more formal partner relationship.

## 5.6 Nom Admin / Partner Manager

This is the internal Nom user managing the program.

They need to:

- approve partners
- verify leads
- assign deals
- review commissions
- approve payouts
- monitor partner quality
- manage training/certification
- handle disputes
- protect Nom’s brand
- ensure restaurants are set up properly

## 5.7 Restaurant Owner / Restaurant Manager

The restaurant is the customer.

They need to:

- trust the partner
- understand Nom’s value
- know what they are buying
- know who will set it up
- receive a proper onboarding experience
- understand pricing and services
- feel supported after going live

# 6. Core Program Promise

The public promise to partners should be clear:

> **Sell Nom to restaurants. Help them get started. Earn commissions from every successful restaurant you bring.**

The expanded promise:

> **Nom gives you the product, training, sales material, tracking, and commission system. You bring restaurant relationships, help them adopt Nom, and earn when they become paying customers.**

The promise should avoid unrealistic get-rich language.

Bad:

> “Earn unlimited money with zero effort.”

Good:

> “If you know restaurant owners and can help them adopt Nom, you can earn through every successful onboarding.”

Best:

> “Become a Nom Partner. Bring restaurants onto Nom, help them set up their operations, and earn from subscriptions, setup services, and add-ons.”

---

# 7. High-Level Partner Journey

The complete partner journey should look like this:

1. Partner discovers the program.
2. Partner applies or signs up.
3. Partner chooses their role: affiliate, sales partner, setup partner, full-service partner.
4. Partner completes onboarding.
5. Partner gets approved or activated.
6. Partner receives sales material and referral tools.
7. Partner identifies a restaurant.
8. Partner registers the restaurant lead.
9. Nom verifies the lead.
10. Partner pitches or introduces Nom.
11. Demo happens.
12. Deal is won or lost.
13. Restaurant subscribes or pays setup/service fee.
14. Partner helps with onboarding/setup if qualified.
15. Restaurant goes live.
16. Partner commission becomes pending.
17. Commission is approved after conditions are met.
18. Partner gets paid.
19. Partner sees performance and next steps.
20. Partner repeats with more restaurants.
21. Partner unlocks higher tier/certification.

This is the core loop.

# 13. Detailed Workflow: Partner Sells Nom

## 13.1 Sales Modes

A partner can sell in different ways.

### Mode 1: Simple Referral

Partner submits lead and Nom handles sales.

Partner earns referral commission if the restaurant becomes eligible.

### Mode 2: Warm Introduction

Partner introduces Nom to owner via WhatsApp/call.

Nom handles demo and closing.

Partner earns higher or better-tracked commission.

### Mode 3: Partner-Led Pitch

Partner explains Nom, qualifies restaurant, and books demo.

Nom may join for final demo/pricing.

Partner earns sales-influenced commission.

### Mode 4: Full Partner Sale

Certified partner handles pitch, demo, closing support, and setup.

Partner earns higher commission and implementation income.

## 13.2 Partner Sales Flow

Step-by-step:

1. Partner identifies restaurant.
2. Partner checks if restaurant fits Nom.
3. Partner registers lead.
4. Partner sends intro/pitch.
5. Partner captures interest.
6. Partner books demo or requests Nom follow-up.
7. Restaurant attends demo.
8. Restaurant receives pricing/proposal.
9. Partner follows up.
10. Deal closes.
11. Restaurant pays.
12. Setup begins.
13. Partner earns after conditions.

## 13.3 Qualification Questions

Partners should be guided to ask:

- How do you currently handle billing/POS?
- Do you track inventory daily?
- Do you use QR menu?
- Do customers order from table or counter?
- Do you have online ordering?
- How many staff use the system?
- Do you manage menu updates manually?
- Are you using Swiggy/Zomato heavily?
- Do you want direct orders?
- How many outlets do you have?
- Who decides on software?
- When are you looking to switch/start?

## 13.4 Sales Statuses

Deal statuses should be clear:

1. Lead Submitted
2. Lead Accepted
3. Intro Sent
4. Contacted
5. Interested
6. Demo Scheduled
7. Demo Completed
8. Pricing Shared
9. Follow-Up Needed
10. Won
11. Lost
12. Dormant

## 13.5 Lost Deal Handling

If deal is lost, partner should know why.

Reasons:

- price too high
- not ready
- using another system
- no response
- bad fit
- owner not interested
- needs future follow-up
- went with competitor

If future follow-up is possible, system should show:

> Follow up after 30 days.

This lets partners continue nurturing.

# 15. Detailed Workflow: Partner Helps Set Up Restaurant

## 15.1 Why Setup Matters

Restaurants may fail if setup is poor.

Good setup increases:

- activation
- retention
- restaurant satisfaction
- partner credibility
- upsell potential
- referrals

Nom should allow qualified partners to earn by helping with setup.

This is different from normal affiliate programs and can become a major advantage.

## 15.2 Setup Partner Eligibility

Not every affiliate can do setup.

To perform setup, partner should become certified or approved.

Requirements may include:

- complete setup training
- pass checklist/quiz
- complete supervised setup
- maintain quality score
- avoid serious complaints

## 15.3 Setup Assignment

Setup can be assigned in different ways:

### Same Partner Setup

The partner who sold the restaurant also sets it up.

Best for full-service partners.

### Nom Team Setup

Nom handles setup.

Partner only earns sales/referral commission.

### Separate Implementation Partner

A setup-certified partner is assigned.

Sales partner earns sales commission.
Implementation partner earns setup commission.

### Hybrid Setup

Partner helps locally; Nom supports remotely.

Useful for early stage.

## 15.4 Setup Checklist

The setup flow should be extremely clear.

Restaurant setup checklist:

### Business Profile

- restaurant name
- address
- contact details
- business hours
- tax/GST settings if relevant
- outlet details

### Menu Setup

- menu categories
- item names
- item descriptions
- prices
- variants
- add-ons
- availability
- taxes
- images, optional

### POS Setup

- billing settings
- payment methods
- receipt settings
- printer settings if relevant
- cashier access
- order types

### QR Menu Setup

- table QR generation
- menu preview
- branding/logo
- customer-facing view
- testing scan flow

### QR Ordering Setup

- customer order flow
- waiter approval flow, if applicable
- payment mode
- kitchen flow
- order notification flow

### Inventory Setup

- item categories
- raw materials
- units
- stock opening values
- recipes/BOM if supported

# 17. Commission Models

The commission system must be simple enough to understand but flexible enough to reward different contributions.

## 17.1 Commission Types

### Referral Commission

Earned when partner refers a restaurant that becomes paying customer.

Best for affiliates.

### Sales Commission

Earned when partner actively helps close the deal.

Best for sales partners.

### Setup Commission

Earned when partner completes approved setup/onboarding.

Best for implementation partners.

### Recurring Commission

Earned as a percentage of subscription revenue for a defined period.

Best for serious partners/resellers.

### Add-On Commission

Earned when partner sells extra modules/services.

Best for full-service partners.

### Bonus Commission

Earned for milestones.

Examples:

- 5 successful restaurants in a month
- 10 restaurants total
- first annual plan sold
- first inventory add-on sold
- locality milestone

## 17.2 Recommended Early Commission Structure

For v1, keep it simple.

Example structure:

### Basic Affiliate

- fixed commission when referred restaurant completes first paid month
- no setup commission unless certified

### Sales Partner

- higher fixed commission or percentage if they actively help close
- must register lead and document involvement

### Setup Partner

- fixed setup payout after approved onboarding
- may be assigned setups separately from sales

### Full-Service Partner

- sales commission + setup commission
- possible recurring commission after proven quality

### Agency/Reseller

- custom terms
- may include recurring commission and implementation revenue

## 17.3 Commission Statuses

Each commission should have a clear lifecycle:

1. Estimated
2. Pending
3. Under Review
4. Approved
5. Scheduled for Payout
6. Paid
7. Held
8. Rejected
9. Reversed

## 17.4 Commission Eligibility

Commission should become eligible only after defined conditions.

Possible conditions:

- restaurant made successful payment
- restaurant did not cancel/refund during validation period
- restaurant completed onboarding
- lead was valid and accepted
- partner complied with rules
- no duplicate/dispute issue

For setup commission:

- setup checklist completed
- restaurant approved setup
- Nom admin approved setup

## 17.5 Commission Explanation

Partners should see why they earned what they earned.

Example:

> You earned ₹2,000 because ABC Café became a paying Nom customer and completed the 30-day validation period.

Example:

> Your setup commission is pending because restaurant approval is not completed yet.

Example:

> This commission is on hold because the restaurant payment failed.

Transparency is critical.

# 22. Rules and Trust

## 22.1 Lead Ownership Rules

Clear lead ownership prevents fights.

Possible rules:

- first accepted valid lead owns opportunity for a defined period
- duplicate leads reviewed by timestamp and quality
- Nom can reject leads already in pipeline
- partner must have meaningful relationship or involvement
- lead ownership may expire if no progress

## 22.2 Lead Expiry

A lead should not be locked forever.

Example:

- lead ownership lasts 60 or 90 days
- if no activity, it can expire
- if deal revives later, admin can decide credit

## 22.3 Duplicate Lead Handling

If two partners submit same restaurant:

- check who submitted first
- check who had stronger relationship
- check whether one only submitted scraped info
- check who created actual introduction
- admin may split commission if both contributed

## 22.4 Self-Referral Rules

If a restaurant owner signs up as partner and refers their own restaurant, this should usually not count unless Nom intentionally runs such an offer.

Need clear policy.

## 22.5 Misrepresentation Rules

Partners must not:

- lie about pricing
- promise features that do not exist
- claim to be Nom employees unless approved
- collect money personally without authorization
- misuse Nom brand
- spam restaurants
- submit fake leads
- harass prospects

Violations can lead to:

- warning
- commission hold
- partner suspension
- termination

## 22.6 Brand Protection

Partners should have approved materials.

This reduces wrong promises.

Materials:

- official pitch deck
- pricing guidelines

- demo videos
- brand usage rules

# 31. Product Philosophy

The first version of the Nom Partner Program should focus on the most commercially powerful loop:

> Partner brings restaurant → restaurant buys Nom → partner helps setup → restaurant goes live → partner earns → partner repeats.

This is the foundation.

Everything else can come later.

The long-term vision is a full restaurant growth network, but the immediate product should be disciplined.

Do not start with every possible referral type.
Do not build a generic affiliate link tool.
Do not focus on vanity sharing.

Build a serious partner system for people who can sell and implement restaurant software.

The program wins when:

- partners trust the commission system
- restaurants trust the partner
- Nom trusts the quality of customers and setups
- everyone knows the next step
- payouts are clear
- setup is done properly
- partners can grow from casual affiliates into certified local operators

The best version of this program does not feel like:

> “Share a link and maybe earn something.”

It feels like:

> “Nom gives me a real product to sell, a clear way to track deals, a way to help restaurants go live, and a serious path to earning more as I become better.”
